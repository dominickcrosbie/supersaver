Understanding Your Calculation

How Superannuation Works

Superannuation is a retirement savings scheme in Australia where employers contribute a percentage of your salary (currently 10.5%) to a superannuation fund on your behalf. When you leave Australia as a temporary resident, you may be eligible to withdraw these funds through the DASP scheme.

Tax Rates Explained

Different visa types have different tax rates applied to super withdrawals:

  • Working Holiday (417/462): 35% tax on withdrawal
  • Student Visa (500): 35% tax on withdrawal
  • Skilled Visa (189/190/491): 20% tax on withdrawal
  • Temporary Work (482): 20% tax on withdrawal
Tips for Accurate Calculation
  • Include all years you worked in Australia
  • Use your gross annual salary (before tax)
  • Select the visa type you held during each year
  • If you had multiple jobs, add up the total salary for each year
  • If your visa changed types during the year, split the salary accordingly
